A gentle hand slowly descended
like an earth-longing leaf
to brush my skin.

A quiet cheek,
cool and compassionate,
bent down and barely touched mine.

And he whispered
like a quiet prayer:
Wife, sad wife.

He had wanted to calm,
he had meant to soothe
the wailing of my flesh.

Marcia Falk
